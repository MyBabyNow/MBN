How much to feed and calculator

How will I know if my baby is getting enough formula?

Your baby's weight gain and the number of wet and dirty nappies will help to tell you if your baby is getting enough formula.

Weight
Your maternal and child health nurse will keep track of and discuss the progress of your baby's weight.

If you're worried about your baby's weight gain, you might find our section on growth charts and monitoring useful.

Wet and dirty nappies
For the first few days after birth, your baby should be producing up to 6 wet nappies soaked through with clear or pale yellow urine a day and pass dark sticky poos (known as 'meconium').

After the first week, your baby should pass pale yellow or yellowish-brown poos.

Tips:

  • Infants have no control over their poos and wee so be cautious when changing a nappy - you might get wet!
  • Bowel habits vary from baby to baby, but generally, formula-fed babies will have more solid poos, less often, than breastfed babies.
  • If your baby has hard and dry poos or has blood in their poo, speak to your GP or maternal and child health nurse for advice.

Formula feeding calculator

Our formula feeding calculator can give you some idea about how much formula to offer at each feed. This is based on your baby's weight, age and the number of feeds you usually offer in a 24-hour period.

Remember:

  • This is a rough guide only and different babies drink different amounts of formula and the amount your baby drinks may vary from day-to-day.
  • Don't worry if your baby doesn't finish the bottle your baby will decide when they are full.
  • How often your baby feeds may also vary; watch for you baby's hungry signs as a guide about when to feed.

Approximate formula requirements for infants in a 24-hour period (includes night feeds) are as follows:

  • Day 5 to 3 months: 150mL for every kg your baby weighs, per day. Some babies, especially those pre-term, need 180-200mL per kg of the baby's weight, per day.
  • 3-6 months: 120mL for every kg your baby weighs, per day.
  • 6-12 months: 100mL for every kg your baby weighs, per day. Some babies will only need 90mL for every kg they weigh, per day. Babies at this age will also be eating solid foods.
