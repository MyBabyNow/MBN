Formula feeding

Out and about

When away from home

Rather than make up a feed and then take it with you, it is much safer to prepare a bottle of formula once you're out, just before your baby needs a feed.

To do so, make sure to bring with you a:

  • measured amount of formula powder in a small, clean and dry container
  • sterilised feeding bottle with a cap that is either empty, or filled with cooled, boiled water (if you get really stuck and didn't get a chance to boil water, you could use unopened bottled still water)

Formula that has already been made up must be stored correctly. If you have access to a refrigerator (to keep formula at or below 5℃) it formula will last up to 24 hours. It's also a good idea to store sterilised bottles containing previously boiled fresh water in the fridge to use as needed.

If your baby needs their bottle warmed, stand the bottle in a container of warm water or warm the water first before adding the formula (if you're making it up). Just make sure you avoid using microwaves to warm bottles as heating isn't always even and can cause 'hot spots' that can burn your baby's mouth and tongue.

Ready to feed formula is another option when sterilisation is not possible.

Transporting a feed

If taking made up formula, prepare a feed as normal and store it in the fridge as per the guidelines - no longer than 1 hour after making it and for no longer than 24 hours.

Only take the made up formula out of the fridge just before you leave (try to have it icy cold) and carry in a cool bag with an ice pack - use within 2 hours. Rewarm the feed (if desired) at your destination.

Try to bring one or more separate sterilised feeding bottles of cooled boiled water at the correct volume so that you can make up more formula as needed, especially if there's a chance you might be away from home for longer than you think.

Bacteria that can harm your baby breed quickly, especially at room temperature, which is why it's so important that made up formula must be stored correctly. If you do not have access to a fridge, or an ice pack, the made up formula must be used within 1 hour or thrown away.
