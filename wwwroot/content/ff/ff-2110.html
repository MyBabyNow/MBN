Tips for parents

Getting support with feeding

Having support with feeding is important for all parents. This might mean your partner shares the load when it comes to your baby's feeding routine or it could mean seeking professional help - either is completely normal. Your maternal and child health nurse can also help with answering any questions you might have regarding formula feeding. You might also like to take our formula preparation quiz and our bottle feeding quiz for personalised feedback and great tips

Use the steps to feeding your baby with a bottle as a guide. It might take a bit of effort to start with but keep following these steps and it will soon become much easier.

Managing feelings of guilt

It doesn't matter what type of feeding you have chosen or the reasons why - the decision you've made was the right one at the time and you have done the best job you can with feeding so far.

Women often feel guilty if they are unable to, or have chosen not to breastfeed, but if you fall into this category, you don't need to feel guilty because your decisions were based on the best possible support and information you had at the time.

You also don't need to feel this way alone - it is important to discuss any feelings of guilt with loved ones or with other new parents or even your GP or maternal and child health nurse for more professional advice.

Remember, when your baby is loved and nourished, whether that is by breastfeeding, formula feeding, or both, you are the best parent they could have.

Dealing with negativity

Sometimes parents who formula feed might face negativity from others, including other parents, friends or family. Just remember that your decision to formula feed your baby is your own - and you are doing the best job possible being a new parent!

A happy, unstressed parent is the best parent your baby could have! If you feel that formula feeding best meets your needs then it best meets your baby's needs too.

Managing mixed feeding

Managing mixed feeding can be tricky but it can have its place and many women choose this option for a number of reasons. You might like to take our mixed feeding quiz for feedback on your reasons for mixed feeding.

There are pros and cons to mixed feeding for both you and your baby - our mixed feeding section can help you manage this.
