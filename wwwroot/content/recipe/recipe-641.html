Baby stew (savoury mince)

Good for: the whole family as a cottage pie
Storage: freeze or in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup onion (finely chopped)
  • ½ cup carrot (diced)
  • ½ cup celery (finely chopped)
  • ¼ cup lean mince (kangaroo, lamb or beef)
  • 2 tablespoons canola or olive oil (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried mixed herbs (optional)
  • ½ 400g tin diced tomatoes

Method

  • Heat oil (if using) in a pot over medium-high heat.
  • Fry onion until soft.
  • Add carrot and celery, frying until just softened.
  • Add mince and break up to brown all pieces thoroughly.
  • Add tomatoes and herbs (if using) and stir through.
  • Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes (add a little water to stop mixture drying out if needed).
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving with mashed potato or pasta.
  • Any unused savoury mince can be frozen in ice/cupcake trays or covered and refrigerated for up to 2 days.

To serve the family
For a family of 4 use:

  • 1 cup onion (chopped)
  • 2 cups carrot (diced)
  • 1 cup celery (chopped)
  • 2 cups lean mince (kangaroo,lamb or beef)
  • 3 tablespoons canola or olive oil (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons dried mixed herbs (optional)
  • 1 400g tin diced tomatoes
  • 1kg potatoes (chopped)
  • ½ cup grated cheese

Follow the same method up to step 5, then

  • Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes (add a little water to stop mixture drying out if needed).
  • Preheat oven to 180°C.
  • Meanwhile, cook potatoes in a pot of boiling water for 15 minutes or until tender, then drain and mash.
  • Put mince mixture in an oven-proof dish, top with mashed potato and grated cheese.
  • Bake in oven for 30 minutes or until top is golden.

Basic vegetable stir-fry

Good for: the whole family
Storage: not suitable for freezing
Note: meat can be added if desired; see video of recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 red onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (finely chopped)
  • 1 cup green beans (cut in half)
  • 1 cup cauliflower pieces
  • 1 capsicum (sliced)
  • 1 cup broccoli pieces
  • 1 cup snow peas (halved)
  • 2 tablespoon low salt soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing cooking wine
  • ½ cup water or low salt stock
  • 2 tablespoons rice bran or vegetable oil
  • Chicken or other meat (optional)
  • Brown rice or quinoa (to serve)

Method

  • Prepare brown rice or quinoa according to instructions on the pack.
  • If using meat: slice into small bite sized pieces and stir fry separately in wok with 1 tablespoon of oil on high heat. Remove when meat turns slightly brown and set aside.
  • Heat oil in a wok or frypan to high heat.
  • Fry onion, ginger and garlic for 1 minute.
  • Add vegetables and ½ cup water or low salt stock, cook for 2 minutes.
  • Add browned meat (if using) and stir in low salt soy sauce, oyster sauce and Shaoxing cooking wine, then cook for another minute or until the sauce thickens.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving with brown rice/quinoa.

Beef and green beans

Storage: Freezer friendly
Note: try different minces for variety - lamb or kangaroo work well

Ingredients

  • 1 cup lean mince (kangaroo, beef or lamb)
  • ⅓ cup green beans (trimmed, cut into ½ cm pieces)
  • 1-2 small potatoes (cut into 8 pieces)
  • 3 tablespoons water or milk

Method

  • Add mince to frying pan and fry over medium-high heat (no need for added oil), breaking up to brown all pieces thoroughly.
  • Stir in 1 tablespoon water/milk then turn off heat and set aside.
  • Meanwhile, boil potatoes in a pot for 10 minutes and steam beans until both are tender.
  • Mash potatoes with the remaining water/milk.
  • Mix/mash/puree beans, mince and potatoes to required consistency before serving.
  • Freeze any unused portions in ice/cupcake trays for later.

Chicken Bolognese

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freezer friendly
Note: serve with pasta or mashed potato; see video of recipe

Ingredients

  • 500g lean chicken mince
  • 1 green or red capsicum (finely chopped)
  • 1 small onion (finely diced)
  • 1 clove garlic (finely chopped)
  • 1 cup mushrooms (finely chopped)
  • ½ cup celery (finely diced)
  • 1 400g tin diced tomatoes (no added salt)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste (no added salt)
  • 2 tablespoons parsley or other fresh herbs (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • pasta or mashed potato, to serve

Method

  • Heat olive oil in large frying pan on medium-high heat.
  • Fry onion and garlic until softened and slightly caramelised.
  • Add chicken mince and break up to brown all pieces thoroughly.
  • Add celery, capsicum and mushrooms then cook for a further 4 minutes.
  • Add tomato paste, tinned tomatoes and parsley.
  • Serve with pasta of choice or mashed potato.

Chicken casserole

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freeze or in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered

Ingredients

  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 teaspoons canola or olive oil
  • 4 small chicken breasts (skin removed, diced and checked for bones)
  • 3-4 large carrots (washed and chopped)
  • 3-4 new/baby potatoes (skin on, washed and chopped)
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 1 teaspoon dried mixed herbs

Method

  • Preheat oven to 180°C.
  • Pour boiling water over herbs.
  • Heat oil in a frying pan.
  • Fry onion until soft.
  • Add chicken and brown on all sides.
  • Transfer to casserole dish, pour over the herby water and add carrots and potatoes.
  • Bake in oven for 1 hour or until chicken is cooked through.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving.
  • Any unused casserole can be frozen in ice trays or covered and refrigerated for up to 2 days.

To serve the family
For a family, follow the same method but add extra vegetables (use the same amount of chicken).

Fish and sweet potato mash

Good for: the whole family
Storage: easy and tasty way to serve nutritious fish

Ingredients

  • ½ large sweet potato (peeled, finely chopped)
  • ½ cup chopped tomatoes (any type will do)
  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil
  • 1 small boneless skinless white fish fillet (e.g. sea bass)
  • 3 tablespoons cow's milk
  • ½ teaspoon canola or olive oil margarine
  • ¼ cup grated cheese

Method

  • Boil sweet potatoes in water for 10 minutes or until tender then drain.
  • Heat oil in a frying pan and fry tomatoes for 4 minutes or until soft then set aside.
  • Place fish, milk and margarine in microwave safe dish, cover with lid or plastic wrap (pierced) and cook on high for 2 minutes or until tender.
  • Drain fish but keep the cooking milk.
  • Break up fish and check for bones.
  • In a bowl, place fish, cooking milk, tomatoes, potatoes and cheese and mash/purée to required consistency before serving (add a dash of milk/water if needed).

To serve the family
For a family of 4 use:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes (peeled, finely chopped)
  • 2 cups chopped tomatoes (any type will do)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (or other vegetable oil)
  • 4 small boneless skinless white fish fillet (e.g. sea bass)
  • ½ cup cow's milk
  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil margarine
  • 1 cup grated cheese

Follow the same method up to step 4, THEN:

  • Serve whole fish fillet on top of mashed potato, pour tomatoes over the top and sprinkle with cheese.

Fish patties

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freezer friendly
Note: serve with salad or steamed vegetables; see video of recipe

Ingredients

  • 400g sweet potatoes (peeled, cut into cubes)
  • 150g frozen peas (cooked)
  • 400g salmon or tuna (canned or freshly cooked)
  • 80g multigrain breadcrumbs
  • herbs e.g. parsley or dill (finely chopped)
  • 4 teaspoons baby rice cereal
  • a little canola or olive oil for frying

Method

  • Microwave or boil sweet potatoes until just tender.
  • In a large bowl, mix and mash the potato and peas.
  • Add the salmon/tuna, herbs and baby rice cereal, then mix well (no need to be smooth).
  • Using your hands, form into patties, coating each in breadcrumbs, then place on a plate.
  • Store in the fridge for 20-30 minutes to set.
  • Heat oil in large frying pan on medium-high.
  • Lightly fry patties in batches until golden brown.
  • Drain on kitchen paper then serve with salad or steamed vegetables.

Lamb hotpot

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freeze or keep in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive margarine
  • 1 ½ cups stewing lamb (in chunks)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 medium carrots (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon plain flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon dried mixed herbs
  • 1 whole bay leaf (optional)
  • 1-2 small potatoes (skin on, washed and thinly sliced)

Method

  • Heat oven to 150°C.
  • Heat oil in a large frying pan on medium-high.
  • Fry lamb until brown on all sides.
  • Transfer lamb to casserole dish.
  • Using same frying pan as for lamb, fry carrots and onion until soft.
  • Add flour and cook for 2 minutes whilst stirring.
  • Stir in herbs, water and bay leaf (if using).
  • Bring to boil then pour over lamb in casserole dish.
  • Place slices of potato on top of lamb mix in casserole dish.
  • Put lid on casserole dish (or cover with foil) and bake in oven for about 1 hour 10 minutes.
  • Remove lid/foil, brush potatoes with margarine/oil.
  • Increase temperature to 180°C and bake for another 10 minutes.
    OR
    Place under grill for 5 minutes until golden.
  • Remove a small portion to mash/purée to required consistency before serving then freeze extra in ice/cupcake trays or cover and refrigerate for up to 2 days.

To serve the family
This recipe is designed for a whole family - follow the same method to step 12, THEN:

  • Serve with salad or greens for a balanced meal.

Lentil cottage pie (veg)

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freezer friendly

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 1 cup carrot (skin on, chopped)
  • 1 cup zucchini (skin on, chopped)
  • 1 clove garlic (crushed)
  • 1 400g tin diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato purée or paste
  • ¼ cup frozen peas
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (finely chopped; or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 300mL water
  • 1 tin 400g green/brown lentils (drained and rinsed)
  • 4 cups potato (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil margarine
  • ½ cup cow's milk
  • ⅓ cup grated cheese

Method

  • Preheat oven to 200°C.
  • Heat oil in a large pot on medium-high heat.
  • Fry onion in oil for 1-2 minutes then add carrot, zucchini and garlic and cook for 1 minute
  • Add tomatoes, tomato purée, peas, thyme, water and lentils.
  • Bring to boil then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes (add a little water to stop mixture drying out if needed).
  • Transfer to large casserole dish.
  • Meanwhile, cook potatoes in boiling water until soft.
  • Drain water and mash, stirring in the margarine, milk and cheese.
  • Spread mash over lentil mixture then run a fork across the top to make ridges.
  • Bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden.
  • Remove a small portion to mash/pur√©e to required consistency before serving.
  • Unused cottage pie can be frozen in ice/cupcake trays for later.

To serve the family
This recipe is designed for a whole family - follow the same method to step 10, THEN:

  • Serve with salad or greens for a balanced meal.

Mini meatballs

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freezer friendly
Note: serve with salad or greens for a balanced meal

Ingredients

  • 500g lean minced beef/kangaroo
  • 2 tablespoons parsley (chopped; or 2 teaspoons dried)
  • 2 cloves garlic (finely chopped)
  • 2 teaspoons oregano (or ½ teaspoon dried)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • 1 cup multigrain breadcrumbs
  • 1 zucchini (skin on, grated, excess moisture squeezed out)
  • plain flour, for coating meatballs
  • a little canola or olive oil for frying

Method

  • Heat oven to 180°C.
  • In a large bowl, combine all ingredients.
  • Roll into small balls and then roll in plain flour.
  • Heat oil in frying pan on medium-high.
  • Lightly fry meatballs in batches until just brown.
  • Place on an oven tray then cook in the oven for 15 minutes until heated through.
  • Serve with salad or greens for a balanced meal.

Pumpkin, carrot and parsnip soup

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freezer friendly
Note: be careful when blending hot soup as splashes can burn! See video of recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 clove garlic (crushed)
  • 1 heaped teaspoon cumin
  • 300g pumpkin (cut into large cubes)
  • 3 carrots (skin on, sliced thickly)
  • 2 parsnips (skin on, sliced thickly)
  • 1L no added salt stock
  • a little canola or olive oil
  • Greek yoghurt, to serve

Method

  • . Heat oil in a large pot on medium-high heat.
  • Cook the onion and garlic for 2-3 minutes or until softened and slightly browned.
  • Add all other ingredients (except yoghurt) and bring to the boil.
  • Turn down the heat and simmer for 15 minutes or until everything is soft.
  • Blend soup and serve with a dollop of Greek yoghurt.

Rainbow chips

Good for:finger food; the whole family
Storage: not suitable for freezing
Note: see video of recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 parsnips (skin on, cut into 5cm chip-like strips)
  • 1 sweet potato (peeled, cut into 5cm chip-like strips)
  • 1 beetroot (peeled, cut into 5cm chip-like strips)
  • 2 tablespoons rosemary
  • 2 cloves of garlic (crushed)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Method

  • Heat oven to 200°C.
  • Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  • Mix vegetables with oil, rosemary and garlic, then spread onto prepared baking tray.
  • Bake for 20 minutes and then turn vegetables over and cook for another 15 minutes, or until tender through and browned.

Simple salmon pasta

Good for: quick and tasty meal that provides plenty of protein and vitamins!
Note: if using tinned salmon/tuna, follow the same method but start at step 3 and only add milk at step 6

Ingredients

  • 1 small salmon fillet or a small tin of salmon/tuna in spring water (drained, bones removed)
  • ½ cup small pasta shapes
  • ½ small leek (diced)
  • ½ cup broccoli (chopped)
  • 3 tablespoons cheese (grated)
  • 4 tablespoons light cream
  • 5 tablespoons cow's milk
  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil

Method

  • Place salmon in microwave safe dish with half the milk, cover with lid or plastic wrap (pierced) and cook on high for 2 minutes or until tender.
  • Drain salmon, check for bones, break up and then set aside.
  • Cook pasta according to instructions on the pack, adding broccoli for the last 2 minutes of cooking, then drain and set aside.
  • Heat oil in a pot over medium-high heat.
  • Fry leek until soft.
  • Add salmon and remaining milk, reduce heat then simmer for 3 minutes.
  • Add cooked pasta (and tinned fish if using), then add light cream.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving with cheese sprinkled on top.

Spaghetti Bolognese (incl. veg option)

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freeze or keep in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered
Note: super popular with little people; for vegetarian Bolognese, replace mince at step 3 with a tin of brown lentils (rinsed) and a handful of chopped mushrooms

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic (crushed)
  • 500g lean mince (kangaroo, beef or lamb)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 medium carrot (grated)
  • 1 medium zucchini (grated)
  • 1 440g tin diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon salt reduced tomato purée or paste
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
  • packet spaghetti or other dried pasta
  • cheese (grated) for serving

Method

  • Heat oil in a large frying pan on medium-high heat.
  • Fry onion, garlic, grated carrot and zucchini until soft.
  • Add mince and break up to brown all pieces thoroughly.
  • Add chopped tomatoes, tomato purée/paste and cinnamon (if using), reduce heat then simmer for 20-30 minutes or until sauce is thick.
  • Cook the spaghetti according to the instructions on the pack.
  • Drain water from spaghetti/pasta and mix through sauce.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving sprinkled with cheese.
  • Freeze unused Bolognese in ice/cupcake trays or cover and refrigerate for up to 2 days.

To serve the family
This recipe is designed for a whole family - follow the same method to step 6, THEN:

  • Serve with cheese sprinkled on top.

Sweetcorn and pea risotto

Good for: lunches as well as dinner
Storage: use straight away and throw away any unused risotto, do not store - it is generally best not to reheat cooked rice
Note: the simple ingredients mean it's great for when you haven't had time to go shopping

Ingredients

  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • handful frozen peas
  • handful of frozen corn kernels
  • ½ cup uncooked white rice
  • ¼ cup grated cheese (parmesan if you have it)
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 1 teaspoon dried mixed herbs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or other vegetable oil)

Method

  • Pour boiling water over herbs.
  • Heat oil in a pot on medium-high heat.
  • Fry onion until soft.
  • Add rice and stir for 2 minutes.
  • Pour in a little herby water, stirring until the rice absorbs it before adding more herby water.
  • After about 7 minutes, add corn and peas.
  • Continue adding herby water little-by-little and stirring until there is none left, the rice is soft and sticky and the peas and corn are well cooked (about 15 minutes all up).
  • Stir in cheese until melted.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving.

Zany zucchini pasta

Good for: the whole family
Note: this is a bit like pesto and is great served alongside grilled chicken

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup small pasta shapes (½ cup already cooked)
  • 1 medium zucchini (sliced)
  • 1 teaspoon chives (chopped)
  • 2 teaspoons canola or olive oil
  • ¼ cup cheese (grated)

Method

  • Cook pasta according the instructions on the pack, drain and set aside if required.
  • Place zucchini in microwave safe bowl, cover with lid or plastic wrap (no need to add water) and cook for 2 minutes until soft.
  • Add oil to zucchini and blend until thick.
  • Stir in chives.
  • Toss pasta in zucchini mixture.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving sprinkled with cheese.

To serve the family
For a family of 4, use:

  • 2 cups small pasta shapes
  • 3 medium zucchinis (sliced)
  • 4 teaspoons chives (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons canola or olive oil
  • 1 cup cheese (grated)

Follow the same method and serve sprinkled with cheese after step 5.
