Important tips

Preparing food for your baby doesn't need to cost the earth, be difficult or time consuming, or even that different from what you eat as a family! Below you will find a few helpful tips before coming to a menu of the recipes included in this section. Although each recipe is grouped by suggested meal time, keep in mind that many are suited to other meal times as well and that your baby will probably just be happy to eat, regardless of the type of food they are presented and the various times of the day!

Important tips

What texture is right for my baby?
Just as a variety of different foods is important, so is a variety of age-appropriate textures. Varying the food texture helps your baby learn to chew (even if they don't yet have teeth!) as well as swallow.
Generally, if your baby is

  • 6 - 8 months - use only puréed and mashed foods but move to minced (or mashed with a fork) and chopped foods soon
  • 8 months - begin finger foods.
  • 12 months - your baby can eat the variety of foods eaten by the rest of the family

See the video library for some tips on preparing different textures, including how to make some adult meals age appropriate for your baby.

Keep the cost down

  • fresh fruit and vegetables that are 'in season' are cheaper, tastier and more nutritious
  • if you don't have one of the ingredients listed in a recipe, try substituting ingredients or experimenting, rather than specifically buying the item

Keep it healthy

  • avoid adding sugar, salt (including stocks), extra fats and oils to foods for your baby
  • choose tinned fruit that is ‘unsweetened’ or 'in fruit juice' instead of syrup
  • practice good hygiene by washing hands, using clean utensils and working on a clean counter
  • It is OK to use a little cow's milk in cooking before your baby is 12 months old

Save time and effort

  • for the most part, your baby can eat the same meals as you,meaning you don't need to prepare separate meals - look for those recipes labelled as good for the whole family and follow the instructions at the end to adapt the recipe 'to serve the family'
  • cook up more than you need and keep extras in the fridge or freeze extra for quick easy meals at a later time
  • keep some frozen vegetables in the freezer and tinned fruit in the cupboard as back-ups for when you run out of fresh vegetables and fruit

All recipes have been carefully sourced but keep in mind that:

  • these recipes are guides only - you may like to experiment in your own kitchen to get things right for you and your baby's tastes
  • cooking times (for example) may be slightly different depending on your appliances and/or the ingredients used the number of serves will vary depending on your baby's age
    • These recipes are a great start for ideas but don't be afraid to change them or use any ingredients you already have at home!

Banana Oat Porridge

Good for: formula fed babies or when your baby is 12 months or older because it can be made with cow's milk
Storage: in the fridge for up to 3 days, covered
Note: if you don't have bananas, make cinnamon oats by adding another pinch of cinnamon instead

Ingredients

  • ½ cup 'quick cook' porridge oats
  • 1 cup cow's milk or made up formula
  • 1 small banana (sliced)
  • 1 pinch ground cinnamon
Method
  • Stovetop: place the oats, milk and sliced banana in a pot and bring to the boil, stirring occasionally, then reduce heat and simmer for 2- 4 minutes.
    • OR
  • Microwave: place the oats, milk and sliced banana in microwave safe bowl, cover with lid or plastic wrap (pierced) and cook on high power for 1 minute then a further 4 minutes on medium power.
  • Cook for longer or add more water/milk to adjust the consistency as needed.ore water/milk to adjust the consistency as needed.
  • Remove from the heat or microwave and add cinnamon, stirring well.
  • Once cooled, serve.
  • Any unused porridge can be covered and refrigerated for up to 3 days.

Banana rice Porridge

Good for: introducing solids
Storage: in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered
Note: super easy and bound to be a favourite with your baby

Ingredients

  • 1 small ripe banana
  • 1-2 teaspoons baby rice cereal
  • 2 tablespoons your baby's usual milk (breast/made up formula milk)
Method
  • Mash the banana well.
  • Mix the rice and milk, and stir into the banana
  • Adjust the texture with more milk or rice to make a runnier or firmer purée.
  • Any unused porridge can be covered and refrigerated for up to 2 days.

Bircher muesli

Good for: the whole family Storage: in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered
Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons rolled oats
  • 3 tablespoons cow's milk (or made up formula milk)
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened plain natural yoghurt
  • ½ cup finely chopped dried fruit (e.g. apricots, dates and sultanas)
  • 1 small pear or apple
Method
  • Mix everything together (except the pear or apple).
  • Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  • Before serving, grate the pear or apple (skin on) and stir into the oat mixture.
  • Serve cold in summer or gently warm for winter.

To serve the family
Follow the same method but use the following amounts per adult:

  • 1⁄3 cup oats
  • ½ cup milk (your usual)
  • ¼ cup unsweetened plain natural yoghurt
  • ½ cup water
  • ¼ cup dried fruit

Eggy bread

Good for: breakfast finger food
Note: a real favourite with babies but always cook eggs well

Ingredients

  • 1 slice soft wholemeal bread
  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • 1 teaspoon canola or olive oil margarine
Method
  • Heat margarine in a frying pan.
  • Dip the bread in the egg - coat both sides well.
  • Place bread in the pan and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  • Remove from the heat and drain excess margarine on paper towel.
  • Cut into fingers and cool before serving with small chunks of tomato or banana.

  • Cook for longer or add more water/milk to adjust the consistency as needed.ore water/milk to adjust the consistency as needed.
  • Remove from the heat or microwave and add cinnamon, stirring well.
  • Once cooled, serve.
  • Any unused porridge can be covered and refrigerated for up to 3 days.

Super porridge (wholegrain baby cereal)

Good for: any meal time
Storage: se straight away and throw away any unused porridge, do not store - it is generally best not to reheat cooked rice
Note: add some vegetables and meat/fish for a balanced meal

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup brown rice
  • ¼ cup dried red lentils
Method
  • Cook rice in water according to instructions on the packet and until it is soft then drain well.
  • Wash the lentils well.
  • Cook the lentils in 1 cup of water - bring to the boil then simmer until it looks like soup (add more water or simmer for longer as required).
  • Mix with rice.
  • Once cool enough, serve.
  • For younger babies, purée the mixed rice and lentils in blender with a little water or their usual milk (breastmilk/made up formula) until smooth.

Tasty spinach scramble

Good for: the whole family
Note: : always cook eggs well

Ingredients

  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • 1 tablespoon onion (finely chopped)
  • ¼ cup frozen spinach (defrosted)
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened plain natural yoghurt
Method
  • Heat oil in a small frying pan.
  • Fry the onion for a few minutes or until tender.
  • Mix the beaten egg with yoghurt and spinach.
  • Pour the egg mixture into the pan and cook for a few minutes, stirring until the egg is cooked through.
To serve the family
Follow the same method but allow per adult:
  • 1 ½ eggs
  • 2 tablespoons chopped onion
  • ½ cup spinach

Vegemite toast

Good for: breakfast finger food
Storage: in the fridge for up to 1 day, covered
Note: Vegemite is high in vitamin B and is an Aussie favourite but it is also high in salt so it should always be spread thinly and we shouldn't eat it very often - babies included.

Ingredients

  • 1 slice wholemeal bread
  • a 'smear' of canola or olive oil margarine (if desired)
  • ½ teaspoon Vegemite
Method
  • Lightly toast bread.
  • Spread with margarine (if using) and Vegemite.
  • Cut into 'soldiers' (long strips of toast).
  • Serve
  • Any untouched toast can be offered later in the day as a snack.

Veggie omelette

Good for: any meal time as well as breakfast; the whole family
Note: always cook eggs well

Ingredients

  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • a splash water
  • handful cooked mushrooms or frozen vegetables (leftovers are perfect)
  • handful grated cheese (optional)
  • pinch dried mixed herbs (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon canola or olive oil margarine
Method
  • Melt margarine in a frying pan on medium heat.
  • Beat water and egg with herbs (if using).
  • Add egg mix to hot pan.
  • Cook the egg mix until the bottom has set on the pan.
  • Add the vegetables and sprinkle cheese (if using) over the top.
  • Finish cooking by placing pan under a hot grill for a few seconds if required to cook the egg all the way through (so there are no clear bits of egg).
To serve the family
Follow the same method but allow per adult:
  • 1 - 1½ eggs
    • AND
  • for every extra adult: double the amount of water and vegetables

Broccoli and polenta mash

Good for: other meal times as well as lunch
Storage: not suitable for freezing
Note: quick and easy

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup fine polenta/semolina
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup cow's milk (or made up formula for babies younger than 12 months)
  • 2 large florets (pieces) broccoli
Method
  • In a pot over medium-high heat, mix polenta with water and milk, stirring continuously until it begins to boil
  • Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes while stirring, until cooked (should be smooth rather than 'grainy' when cooked).
  • Meanwhile, steam/boil/microwave broccoli until tender.
  • Mash / purée broccoli and polenta together to required consistency before serving.

Cheesy leek and potato mash

Good for: the whole family
Storage: freeze or keep in the fridge for up to 3 days, covered

Ingredients

  • ½ cup leek (finely chopped)
  • 1 small potato (diced)
  • ¼cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon canola or olive oil margarine
  • boiling water
Method
  • Melt margarine in a frying pan
  • Fry leek over a low heat for 3 minutes.
  • Add potato, cover with boiling water and cook for 6 minutes or until soft.
  • Drain excess water.
  • Add cheese and mash to the required consistency.
  • Use once cooled
  • Freeze any extra in ice trays for later.
To serve the family
For a family of 4 use:
  • 2 leeks (finely chopped)
  • 5 potatoes (diced)
Follow the same method up to step 4, then
  • Put into a baking dish (do not mash).
  • Sprinkle with cheese.
  • Bake for 20 minutes at 180°C or until golden.
  • Purée or mash for your baby with some of your baby's usual milk (breast/formula milk).

Cheesy pasta sauce

Good for: basis for macaroni/cauliflower cheese or lasagne topping; the whole family
Storage: freeze or keep in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil margarine
  • 1 tablespoon plain flour
  • 2 cups cow's milk (or made up formula for babies less than 12 months old)
  • ½ cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried mixed herbs (optional)
Method
  • Melt margarine in a pot over a low heat.
  • Add flour and herbs (if using), stirring to make smooth paste.
  • Increase heat slightly and add milk, a little at a time, stirring until smooth.
  • Continue stirring and simmer for 5 minutes or until sauce thickens.
  • Remove from heat and stir in cheese until melted.
  • Use or freeze extra in ice/cupcake trays for later.
To serve the family
For a family of 4, follow the same method but instead use:
  • 2 tablespoons canola or olive oil margarine
  • 2 tablespoons plain flour
  • 4 cups cow's milk (for babies old than 12 months)
  • 1 ½cups grated cheddar cheese
  • 2 teaspoons dried mixed herbs (optional)

Cheesy spinach spaghetti

Good for: other meal times as well as lunch; the whole family
Storage: freeze or keep in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered
Note: young people love spaghetti; adding spinach adds extra nutrition

Ingredients

  • about 20 strands thin spaghetti (or 1 cup pasta shapes)
  • 1 tablespoon canola or olive oil
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 clove garlic (crushed)
  • 3 cups fresh spinach/silverbeet (or 50-60g defrosted frozen spinach)
  • 1 ¼ cups plain cottage cheese (blended/mashed)
  • 1 squeeze lemon juice
  • 4 tablespoons grated cheese (parmesan if you have it)
Method
  • Melt margarine in a pot over a low heat.
  • Add flour and herbs (if using), stirring to make smooth paste.
  • Increase heat slightly and add milk, a little at a time, stirring until smooth.
  • Continue stirring and simmer for 5 minutes or until sauce thickens.
  • Remove from heat and stir in cheese until melted.
  • Use or freeze extra in ice/cupcake trays for later.
To serve the family
For a family of 4 use:
  • 500g thin spaghetti (or 3 cups pasta shapes)
  • 3 tablespoons canola or olive oil margarine
  • 3 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 9 cups fresh spinach/silverbeet (or 160g defrosted frozen spinach)
  • 3 ¾cups plain cottage cheese (blended/mashed)
  • 3 squeezes lemon juice
  • ¾cup grated cheese (parmesan if you have it) Follow the same method but serve after step 6.

Dahl

Good for: serve thick with rice for the whole family
Storage: freeze or in the fridge for up to 3 days, covered
Note: easy, cheap and a real winner with little people; makes enough for 1-2 feeds

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon dried red lentils
  • ¼ cup mixed vegetables (e.g. carrot, peas, beans)
  • 2 cups water
  • pinch turmeric or cumin (optional)
Method
  • Wash lentils then soak in water for 30 minutes (to reduce 'gassiness').
  • Drain water then wash again.
  • Put lentils, vegetables, water and turmeric/cumin (if using) in a pot on high heat.
  • Bring to the boil then reduce heat and simmer for 30 - 40 minutes, stirring often to stop lentils sticking to the bottom.
  • When lentils and vegetables are soft enough to mash with a spoon, remove from heat.
  • Mash/Purée to required consistency with extra water if needed before serving warm
  • Any unused dahl can be frozen or covered and refrigerated for up to 3 days.
To serve the family
For a family of 4 use:
  • 1 ½ cups dried red lentils
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (e.g. carrot, peas, beans)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons canola or olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 2 cups dry brown/basmati rice
Follow the same method up to step 3, then
  • Fry turmeric and cumin in oil in a pot for a few seconds then add lentils and water.
  • Bring to the boil then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes,stirring often to stop lentils sticking to the bottom.
  • Add vegetables and simmer for another 5 minutes (add more water if needed).
  • Cook rice according to the instructions on pack.
  • Serve Dahl with rice.

Eggy veg

Good for: other meal times as well as lunch
Storage: in the fridge for up to 1 day, covered; not suitable for freezing
Note: great way to use leftover vegetables; always cook eggs well
Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons plain cottage cheese
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped cooked vegetables (e.g. carrots, green beans, broccoli)
Method
  • Preheat oven to 180°C.
  • In a bowl, beat egg with cottage cheese.
  • Stir in chopped vegetables.
  • Grease a small oven-proof dish (at least 200mL) and pour in egg mixture.
  • Place dish on baking tray and cook for about 20 minutes or until egg is set.

Mushroom carbonara

Good for: any meal time; the whole family
Storage: freezer friendly
Note: tasty, healthier dish that has much less salt than the traditional ham/bacon version

Ingredients

  • about 20 strands uncooked thin spaghetti (or 1 cup pasta shapes)
  • 1 teaspoon corn flour
  • ½ cup mushrooms (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon canola or olive oil
  • ½ cup cow's milk
  • ½handful grated cheese
  • ½ cup frozen peas
  • pinch dried mixed herbs (optional)
Method
  • Cook spaghetti/pasta according to the instructions on the pack.
  • Meanwhile, fry mushrooms in oil on medium heat until soft.
  • In a mug, mix corn flour with a few teaspoons of milk until combined and add to mushrooms
  • Stir remaining milk, frozen peas and herbs (if using) into mushrooms.
  • Reduce heat and stir for 2 minutes or until the sauce bubbles and thickens.
  • Remove from heat and set aside.
  • Stir cooked spaghetti/pasta and cheese through mushroom sauce.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving.
  • Any unused carbonara can be frozen in ice trays for later.
To serve the family
For a family of 4 use:
  • 500g thin spaghetti (or 3 cups pasta shapes)
  • 2 cups onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons canola or olive oil
  • 4 teaspoons corn flour
  • 1 ½ cups milk
  • 1 cup grated cheese
  • 2 cups frozen peas
  • 1 teaspoon dried mixed herbs (optional)
Follow the same method (serve after step 8).

Salmon and veggie couscous

Good for: quick and easy meal anytime; the whole family
Note: if using tinned salmon/tuna, follow the same method but cook just the tomatoes and capsicum in foil at step 3

Ingredients

  • 1 small skinless salmon fillet or a small tin of salmon/tuna in spring water (drained)
  • ½ medium capsicum (finely chopped)
  • 3 medium cherry tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • ¼ cup uncooked couscous (or white rice)
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • ½ teaspoon canola or olive oil margarine
  • ½ teaspoon lemon juice (optional)
Method
  • Preheat oven to 180°C.
  • Place salmon on a flat piece of foil (if using fresh fish).
  • Top salmon with tomatoes and capsicum then sprinkle with lemon juice if using.
  • Seal salmon parcel in foil and place on baking tray.
  • Cook for 20-30 minutes or until salmon is cooked through.
  • Flake and mash salmon, making sure there are no bones.
  • Place couscous in a bowl (if using rice, cook according to instructions on the pack).
  • Add boiling water until just covered and mix in the margarine.
  • Cover and leave for 5 minutes.
  • Use a fork to 'fluff up' the couscous.
  • Mix the salmon/tinned fish and vegetables with couscous/rice.
  • Mash/ purée to required consistency before serving.
To serve the family
Follow the same method but serve after step 12 and allow per adult:
  • 1 medium skinless salmon fillet
  • ½ medium capsicum (finely chopped)
  • 5 medium cherry tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1⁄3 cup uncooked couscous (or white rice)
  • ½ teaspoon canola or olive oil margarine
  • ½ teaspoon lemon juice (optional)

Sweet potato and lentil soup

Good for: any meal time; the whole family as a soup
Storage: freezer friendly
Note: hearty, cheap and provides good protein and energy

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon canola or olive oil
  • 1 large carrot (washed and chopped small)
  • 1⁄3 cup dried red lentils
  • 2 small sweet potatoes (peeled, chopped into chunks)
  • 1 whole bay leaf (optional)
  • 2 cups hot water
  • pinch dried mixed herbs (optional)
Method
  • Wash lentils well.
  • Put oil, carrots and lentils in a pot and cook on medium heat for 2 minutes
  • Add sweet potato, water, bay leaf (if using) and herbs (if using).
  • Turn heat up until it boils, then lower heat and simmer for 30 minutes or until lentils and vegetables are soft.
  • Remove bay leaf (if used).
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving.
  • Freeze any unused soup in ice trays for later.
To serve the family
For a family of 4, use:
  • 2 teaspoons canola or olive oil
  • 3 large carrots (chopped small)
  • 1 ½ cup dried red lentils
  • 4 small sweet potatoes (peeled, chopped into chunks)
  • 2 whole bay leaves (optional)
  • 5 cups hot water (add more if needed)
  • ½ teaspoon dried mixed herbs (optional)
Follow the same method to step 5 and serve chunky or blend for a smooth soup.

Tuna and creamed corn macaroni

Good for: any meal time
Storage: freezer friendly
Note: : babies and young children love tuna; great source of iron and calcium

Ingredients

  • ½ cup macaroni (or other pasta)
  • 2 tablespoons tinned tuna in spring water (drained)
  • 2 tablespoons creamed corn
  • ¼ cup grated cheese
  • 2 tablespoons cow's milk (or breast/made up formula)
Method
  • Cook macaroni/pasta according to the instructions on pack.
  • Drain water from macaroni then return to pot.
  • Over medium heat, add milk and cheese, stirring until combined and cheese has melted.
  • Stir in creamed corn and tuna.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving warm
  • Freeze any unused macaroni in ice trays for later.

Baby stew (savoury mince)

Good for: the whole family as a cottage pie
Storage: freeze or in the fridge for up to 2 days, covered

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup onion (finely chopped)
  • ½ cup carrot (diced)
  • ½ cup celery (finely chopped)
  • ¼ cup lean mince (kangaroo, lamb or beef)
  • 2 tablespoons canola or olive oil (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried mixed herbs (optional)
  • ½ 400g tin diced tomatoes
Method
  • Heat oil (if using) in a pot over medium-high heat.
  • Fry onion until soft
  • Add carrot and celery, frying until just softened.
  • Add mince and break up to brown all pieces thoroughly.
  • Add tomatoes and herbs (if using) and stir through.
  • Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes (add a little water to stop mixture drying out if needed).
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving with mashed potato or pasta.
  • Any unused savoury mince can be frozen in ice/cupcake trays or covered and refrigerated for up to 2 days.
To serve the family
For a family of 4 use:
  • 1 cup onion (chopped)
  • 2 cups carrot (diced)
  • 1 cup celery (chopped)
  • 2 cups lean mince (lamb or beef)
  • 3 tablespoons canola or olive oil (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons dried mixed herbs (optional)
  • 1 400g tin diced tomatoes
  • 1kg potatoes (chopped)
  • ½ cup grated cheese
Follow the same method up to step 5, then

  • Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes (add a little water to stop mixture drying out if needed).
  • Preheat oven to 180°C.
  • Meanwhile, cook potatoes in a pot of boiling water for 15 minutes or until tender, then drain and mash.
  • Put mince mixture in an oven-proof dish, top with mashed potato and grated cheese.
  • Bake in oven for 30 minutes or until top is golden.

Basic vegetable stir-fry

Good for: the whole family
Storage: not suitable for freezing
Note: meat can be added if desired

Ingredients

  • 1 red onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (finely chopped)
  • 1 cup green beans (cut in half)
  • 1 cup cauliflower pieces
  • 1 capsicum (sliced)
  • 1 cup broccoli pieces
  • 1 cup snow peas (halved)
  • 2 tablespoon low salt soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoon Shaoxing cooking wine
  • ½ cup water or low salt stock
  • 2 tablespoons rice bran or vegetable oil
  • Chicken or other meat (optional)
  • Brown rice or quinoa (to serve)
Method
  • Prepare brown rice or quinoa according to instructions on the pack.
  • If using meat: slice into small bite sized pieces and stir fry separately in wok with 1 tablespoon of oil on high heat. Remove when meat turns slightly brown and set aside.
  • Heat oil in a wok or frypan to high heat.
  • Fry onion, ginger and garlic for 1 minute.
  • Add vegetables and ½ cup water or low salt stock, cook for 2 minutes.
  • Add browned meat (if using) and stir in low salt soy sauce, oyster sauce and Shaoxing cooking wine, then cook for another minute or until the sauce thickens.
  • Mash/purée to required consistency before serving with brown rice/quinoa.

Beef and green beans

Storage: Freezer friendly
Note: try different minces for variety - lamb or kangaroo work well

Ingredients

  • 1 cup lean mince (kangaroo, beef or lamb)
  • 1⁄3 cup green beans (trimmed, cut into ½ cm pieces)
  • 1-2 small potatoes (cut into 8 pieces)
  • 3 tablespoons water or milk
Method
  • Add mince to frying pan and fry over medium-high heat (no need for added oil), breaking up to brown all pieces thoroughly.

Drinks

  • Milk
  • Water
  • Fruit juice
  • Cordial / fizzy drinks / soft-drinks / energy drinks

Milk

The type of milk your baby should have depends on their age. Breastmilk or formula provides all the nutrition your baby needs up to around 6 months. The following guidelines should be followed unless your GP or maternal and child health nurse advises you otherwise:

  • from 8 months, offer your baby breastmilk/formula after solids meals to make sure they are hungry enough to eat the new iron rich foods they now need
  • from 6 months, if you are formula feeding, you may wish to offer formula in a sippy cup rather than a bottle.
  • cow's milk should not be given as a drink until your baby is older than 12 months but small quantities used in cooking are OK
  • until your baby is 2 years or older, always provide regular full cream cow's milk (instead of low/reduced fat cow's milk as this doesn't contain enough energy for your growing baby)

Water

  • If your baby is under 6 months old, they will get all the hydration they need from breastmilk/formula (even in hot weather) and should not be given water.
  • From 6 months it is OK to give your baby small sips of cooled boiled tap water if they seem thirsty (e.g. in hot weather) but this shouldn't be done regularly as it can fill your baby's little tummy, making them feel too full to drink enough milk to get the energy they need to grow.
  • It's a good idea to encourage your baby to drink (milk, or water if having) from a cup after 6 months of age!

Fruit juice

Fruit juice should not form part of your baby's diet for a few reasons:

  • compared with the whole fruit, it's far easier to drink too much which can cause your baby to gain too much weight
    • it takes about 3 whole oranges to make just 1 cup of orange juice
    • there is no chewing involved, so it can be swallowed quickly
    • most of the fibre is lost so it passes through more quickly
  • too much can cause diarrhoea
  • the natural sugars can cause tooth decay - even before the teeth have come through

Cordial / fizzy drinks / soft-drinks / energy drinks

Cordials, fizzy drinks, soft-drinks and energy drinks should never be given to your baby as they can damage growing teeth and cause your baby to gain too much weight. Energy drinks also contain high levels of caffeine which can be harmful to your baby's developing body.
