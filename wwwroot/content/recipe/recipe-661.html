Drinks

  • Milk
  • Water
  • Fruit juice
  • Cordial / fizzy drinks / soft drinks / energy drinks

Milk

The type of milk your baby should have depends on their age. Breastmilk or formula provides all the nutrition your baby needs up to around 6 months.
The following guidelines should be followed unless your GP or maternal and child health nurse advises you otherwise:

  • from 8 months, offer your baby breastmilk/formula after solids meals to make sure they are hungry enough to eat the new iron rich foods they now need
  • from 6 months, if you are formula feeding, you may wish to offer formula in a sippy cup rather than a bottle
  • cow's milk should not be given as a drink until your baby is older than 12 months but small quantities used in cooking are OK
  • until your baby is 2 years or older, always provide regular full cream cow's milk (instead of low/reduced fat cow's milk as this doesn't contain enough energy for your growing baby)

Water

If your baby is under 6 months old, they will get all the hydration they need from breastmilk/formula (even in hot weather) and should not be given water.

From 6 months it is OK to give your baby small sips of cooled boiled tap water if they seem thirsty (e.g. in hot weather) but this shouldn't be done regularly as it can fill your baby's little tummy, making them feel too full to drink enough milk to get the energy they need to grow.

It's a good idea to encourage your baby to drink (milk, or water if having) from a cup after 6 months of age!

Fruit juice

Fruit juice should not form part of your baby's diet for a few reasons:

  • compared with the whole fruit, it's far easier to drink too much which can cause your baby to gain too much weight
    • it takes about 3 whole oranges to make just 1 cup of orange juice
    • there is no chewing involved, so it can be swallowed quickly
    • most of the fibre is lost so it passes through more quickly
  • too much can cause diarrhoea
  • the natural sugars can cause tooth decay - even before the teeth have come through

Cordial / fizzy drinks / soft drinks / energy drinks

Cordials, fizzy drinks, soft-drinks and energy drinks should never be given to your baby as they can damage growing teeth and cause your baby to gain too much weight. Energy drinks also contain high levels of caffeine which can be harmful to your baby's developing body.
