The peak body for Australian and New Zealand professionals involved in lactation and breastfeeding. Use the find a lactation consultant page to locate a lactation consultant in your local area.

Website

Your local hospital / birth centre
If you are yet to give birth, ask if your hospital / birth centre where you plan to give birth provides breastfeeding support after you go home. For help and advice with breastfeeding contact the hospital you gave birth at (or the midwife who assisted you) to find out if there is a lactation consultant. Many community health centres also offer breastfeeding support services. Your GP or maternal and child health nurse is the most useful contact for information for you and your baby's wellbeing, particularly if you are planning or had a homebirth.

Midwives Australia
The peak body for midwives in Australia can also provide breastfeeding support once you've had your baby. Resources include a comprehensive website with information and advice about midwifery, a find a midwife page to locate privately practising midwives (Medicare eligible) in your local area and links to further resources.

Website

Breastfeeding and medicine

NPS MedicineWise
Provider of expert advice on all medication matters. Look up information on different medications, health topics, conditions and tests available online, or call the Medicines Line to discuss medicines with a professional.

1300 633 424 (9am-5pm Mon-Fri (Medicines Line)
Website

New South Wales

MotherSafe
A service provided by the Royal Hospital for Women, Randwick which provides a comprehensive counselling service for women about exposures (e.g. prescription drugs, over-the-counter medications, street drugs, infections, radiation and occupational exposures) during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

02 9382 6539 (Sydney area) / 1800 647 848 (NSW country) (9am-5pm Mon-Fri)
Website

Victoria

Medicines Information Centre
A telephone service by Monash Health that provides advice on new medicines, side effects and medicine interactions. Specialises in medicines in pregnancy and breastfeeding and for children and women's health.

03 9594 2361 (9am-5pm Mon-Fri)

Medicines Information Service
A telephone and email service by the Royal Women's Hospital that provides advice on medications (including complementary and alternative medicines), tests and everyday items that are safe and those to avoid during pregnancy and while breastfeeding. Website also includes fact sheets on medicines and herbal/traditional medicines during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

03 8345 3190) (8:30am-4pm Mon-Fri)

Dietitian

Dietitians Association of Australia
The peak body for dietitians in Australia. Resources include a comprehensive website with information about healthy eating, recipes and a find an Accredited Practising Dietitian page to locate a dietitian in your local area, including dietitians who specialise in pregnancy, breastfeeding and paediatrics.
