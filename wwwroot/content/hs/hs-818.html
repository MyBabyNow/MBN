Bereavement

Red Nose Grief and Loss
Service dedicated to supporting bereaved families and their support networks for the sudden and unexpected death of a baby or child (including stillbirth, during infancy and childhood). Resources include counselling, parent and family support, peer support, sibling support, grandparent support groups, group activities, annual memorial services, telephone counselling and a national 24 hour free bereavement support line.

1300 308 307) (24 hours/7 days)
Website

Sands Australia
National charity service dedicated to providing support, information and education about miscarriage, stillbirth and neonatal death. Resources include comprehensive website with real life stories from other parents and the wider community, 24 hour support line, email support, local support groups run by bereaved parents and online live chats.

1300 072 637) (24 hours/7 days)
support@sands.org.au
Online chat (time varies with location Mon-Thu)
Website

Carers

Carers Australia
The national peak body representing Australia's carers. Works with the state and territory Carers Associations who are the state-wide voices for family carers by representing and providing support to carers. Resources vary by state but generally include a comprehensive website with information and advice for carers, counselling (referral from a health care professional required), advocacy and carer advice line.

New South Wales

Carers NSW
1800 171 882 (9am-5pm Mon-Fri)
Website

Victoria

Carers Victoria
1800 171 882 (9am-5pm Mon-Fri)
Website

Children and young people

Headspace
Provides people aged 12-25 years (and their friends and families) with information, support and advice on general health, mental health and wellbeing. Resources include a website with information about mental health for those aged 12-25 years, details of headspace centre locations and support via telephone or online - eheadspace chat or email.

1800 650 890 (9am-1am/7 days)
eheadspace Website (9am-1am/7 days)
headspace Website

Kids Helpline
Free, private and confidential, telephone and online counselling service specifically for young people aged 5-25 years (and their parents and carers). Resources include website with information such as peer pressure, different types of families and taking risks, as well as telephone, online and email counselling.

1800 551 800) (24 hours/7 days)
Website

Depression

Beyondblue
Works toward achieving an Australian community that understands depression and anxiety as well as empowering all Australians, at any life-stage, to seek help. Resources include professional support via telephone, online chat (or email), as well as a comprehensive website with information, resources and online forums.

1300 224 636 (24 hours/7 days)
Online chat (3pm-12am/7 days)
Website

Blackdog Institute
Credible and comprehensive website about the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of mood disorders such as depression and bipolar disorder.

Website

PANDA Perinatal Anxiety & Depression Australia
Telephone information, support and referral service for new parents struggling during pregnancy or after the birth of their baby, as well as for family and friends.

1300 726 306) (10am-5pm Mon-Fri)
Website

Physical and sexual assault

1800RESPECT
A telephone and online counselling, information and support service for people experiencing sexual assault, domestic and family violence.

1800 737 732 (24 hours/7 days)
Website

New South Wales

Rape Crisis Centre
A telephone and online crisis counselling service for anyone in New South Wales who has experienced or is at risk of sexual assault and their non-offending supporters.

1800 424 017 (24 hours/7 days)
Website

Victoria

Domestic Violence Resource Centre Victoria
State-wide service providing training, publications, research and other resources to those experiencing (or who have experienced) family violence, as well as those working with family violence survivors. Resources include comprehensive website with advice and resources aimed at various sectors of the community and links to support and services.

Website

Sexual Assault Crisis Line
A state-wide, after-hours telephone crisis counselling service for victim/survivors of both past and recent sexual assault.

1800 806 292 (5pm-9am Mon-Fri; 24 hours Sat-Sun/public holidays)
(TTY 1800 555 677) then ask for 1800 806 292)
Website

Pregnancy counselling

Pregnancy, Birth and Baby
An Australian Government funded telephone and online helpline which supports parents on the journey from pregnancy to preschool. Resources include comprehensive website with reliable, up-to-date information on pregnancy, parenting and health services as well as advice and support by maternal child health nurses.

1800 882 436 (7am-12am/7 days)
Video call (7am-12am/7 days)
Website

Pregnancy Counselling Australia
A telephone service whose female counsellors are trained specifically for pregnancy and abortion counselling.

1300 737 732 (24 hours/7 days)
Website

New South Wales

get healthy
Free personal telephone health information and coaching service provided by NSW Health to help and motivate you to reach your healthy eating, activity and weight goals. Includes a specific get healthy in pregnancy program, as well as a comprehensive website with online calculators, fact sheets (plus pregnancy specific sheets) and links to further resources.

1300 806 258 (8am-8pm Mon-Fri)
Website

Quit smoking

Quitnow
Australian Government initiative to support you to quit smoking. Resources include a comprehensive website with information on why and how to quit, the effects of tobacco smoke (even if second-hand) on your baby during pregnancy and as an infant, interactive tools, phone apps (including Quit for you - Quit for Two, aimed at those who are currently, or are trying to fall, pregnant), telephone Quitline, online QuitCoach and mobile QuitTxt.

13 78 48 (8am-8pm Mon-Fri)
Website

Relationship counselling

MensLine Australia
Professional telephone and online support, information and referral service, helping men to deal with relationship problems. Resources include a comprehensive website with practical information and forums, as well as online, video and telephone counselling.

1300 789 978 (24 hours/7 days)
Online & video counselling (24 hours/7 days)
Website

Relationships Australia
Service for individuals, families and communities aiming to support all people in Australia to achieve positive and respectful relationships. Resources include website with relationship advice as well as details of counselling services and programs.

1300 364 277
Website

Suicide risk

Lifeline
A confidential telephone or one-on-one online crisis support service for anyone experiencing a personal crisis or thinking about suicide.

13 11 14 (24 hours/7 days)
Crisis Support Chat (7pm-4am/7 days)
Website

Suicideprevention.com.au
An information service for anyone thinking about suicide provided by the Australian Suicide Prevention Foundation.

Website

Victoria

SuicideLine
An anonymous counselling service offering professional support to people at risk of suicide, people concerned about someone else's risk of suicide, and people bereaved by suicide.

1300 651 251 (24 hours/7 days)
Website

Pregnancy, Birth and Baby
An Australian Government funded telephone and online helpline providing support and information to both expecting parents and parents of children aged up to 5 years, from a maternal child health nurse. A range of questions you might have about your child's development or behaviour can be answered, such as: sleeping, settling, feeding, toilet training and tantrums.

1800 882 436 (7am-12am/7 days)
Video call (7am-12am/7 days)
Website
