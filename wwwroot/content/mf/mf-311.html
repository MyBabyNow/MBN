Mixed feeding

What is mixed feeding?

Mixed feeding simply means your baby has a combination of both your breastmilk and formula.

There are many reasons why you may choose to mix feed your baby. Whatever the reason, deciding to mix feed is a personal decision that depends on the unique relationship between you and your baby, your family situation and any professional advice that you receive.

If you think mixed feeding may work for you and your baby, it is important to first speak with your GP, maternal and child health nurse, a lactation consultant or call the ABA helpline before making changes.

Take our quiz to find out if mixed feeding is right for your situation.

Is mixed feeding recommended?

Until around 6 months old, it is recommended that giving only breastmilk is healthiest for your baby. After 6 months, it is recommended that babies keep breastfeeding alongside starting solid foods.

Many mums either can't fully breastfeed or choose not to, and therefore replace some breastfeeds with formula instead. Mixed feeding means your baby will still get some breastmilk, which is better than no breastmilk! But keep in mind that replacing some breastfeeds with formula will reduce your milk supply.

If your baby needs extra feeds with formula for their health and growth, or you need to mix feed your baby for your own health and wellbeing, be confident that you are making the right decision for your family.

Remember:

  • regularly giving your baby formula instead of breastmilk means they will miss out on some of the protective benefits of breastmilk and doing so will also reduce your milk supply.
  • thinking about the reasons why your baby might need extra feeds with formula can help you to decide if mixed feeding is right for your situation - find out more by doing our mixed feeding quiz.
