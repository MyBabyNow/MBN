Colour every meal with fruit and vegetables

Making sure every meal includes a variety of colourful fruits and vegetables works on many levels!

A variety of colours:

  • makes the plate look good - meaning we're more likely to want to eat them
  • means a variety of flavours - making it more interesting for our taste buds
  • increases the number of different nutrients in the meal - boosting your health

And, by making sure both yours and your child's meals include a variety of colourful fruits and vegetables, you're also teaching your child about healthy eating, setting them up for a lifetime of healthy habits!

It doesn't matter if colourful fruits and vegetables are fresh, frozen or canned, just as long as they're there! They don't even have to be made into fancy meals - simple and healthy are just fine!

Remember though, it is normal for babies to spit out new and unknown foods, especially vegetables, which tend to be less sweet and more bitter than other foods. In fact, it can take up to 10-15 tries of a new vegetable before your baby may learn to like it! The key is not to give up but to keep offering rejected vegetables, provided you also avoid the food battles.
