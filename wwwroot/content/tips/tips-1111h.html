Parents provide, babies decide

When it comes to feeding your baby, your role is to:

  • recognise and respond to your baby's hungry and fullness signs
  • provide a range of healthy foods (or offer a breastfeed or a bottle)
  • choose where the family will eat

Your baby's role is to:

  • decide if they will eat the food
  • explore their food by playing with it (and maybe try feeding themselves)
  • decide how much of the food they will eat

It's important that you don't force your baby to finish all the food you offer if they don't want to. Letting your baby learn to trust their appetite from an early age will help them not to overeat when they are older.

Remember, no baby will ever starve themselves...and the more you fuss about food, the more they will fuss!

Parents provide, babies decide can also be applied to active play - parents provide a range of active play opportunities and babies decide which activities to engage in!
