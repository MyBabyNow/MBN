Tired signs

There are many ways your baby might show you they are tired. This video shows what tired signs look like in real babies.

Video: Tired signs

Sourced from the Raising Children website, Australia's trusted parenting website. For more parenting information, visit raisingchildren.net.au.

Some signs to look for:

  • pulling at ears
  • closing fists
  • fluttering eyelids
  • jerky arm and leg movements
  • yawning
  • a worried look on their face
  • arching backwards
  • difficulty focusing e.g. cross-eyed or staring into space
  • sucking on fingers
  • grizzly cry

When your baby shows tired signs, settle them for sleep by changing their nappy, wrapping or swaddling them and putting them in their cot to sleep.

Try to put your baby down when they are drowsy but still awake. This will help them learn to fall asleep by themselves. This is a skill that babies need to learn and it will help them become good sleepers in the future.

If you are out when your baby shows tired signs, you can put your baby to sleep in the pram or baby pouch. Walking with the pram or wheeling back and forth can help with settling your baby to sleep.
