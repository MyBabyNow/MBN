Dropping the night feed

From 6 months of age, babies who are developing well no longer need to feed overnight. It is up to you whether you choose to keep going with the night feed or drop it - either is OK. If you are unsure, discuss with your maternal and child health nurse.

How to drop the night feed depends on whether your baby is being breast or formula-fed, as well as how long their night feed currently is.

Breastfed babies

If your baby's night feed is:

  • less than 5 minutes - it can be stopped outright and your baby re-settled to sleep
  • usually longer than 5 minutes - gradually cut back the time you spend feeding over 5-7 nights to help your baby get used to the change

Formula-fed babies

If your baby's night feed is:

  • 60mL of formula or less - it can be stopped outright and your baby re-settled to sleep
  • more than 60mL - gradually cut down on the amount your baby drinks over 5-7 nights to help your baby get used to the change
