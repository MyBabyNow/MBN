Night waking

Has your baby started waking more at night?

It is not uncommon for babies at 3-4 months old to start waking more at night, even if they have been good sleepers until now. This doesn't always mean they are hungry.

Some parents wonder if introducing solid foods will help their baby sleep more at night. But starting solid foods before 6 months is unlikely to stop the night waking as there are other reasons why babies this age start waking up more at night.

Common reasons for night waking include:

  • Sleep cycle
    As babies move from one sleep cycle to another, they may wake and call out for their parents. The trick is to have your baby learn to put themselves back to sleep without your help.
  • Sleep habits
    At 3-4 months of age babies start to learn sleep associations; e.g. always being fed or rocked to sleep, or having a dummy. If they wake between sleep cycles they then need the same things to get back to sleep.
  • Developmental milestones
    Developmental milestones can change sleep and cause your baby to wake up more. For example, when your baby learns to roll, they may get stuck in new positions or they might just want to practice their new skill at night!

Don't worry, there are many ways to encourage your baby to achieve independent sleep!

Independent sleep

A baby has achieved independent sleep when they:

  • sleep for 6-8 hours during the night
  • can settle back to sleep without calling out to a parent after waking in the night

Around 60% of babies can do this by 6 months of age.

Help your baby achieve independent sleep by:

  • putting your baby to bed drowsy, but awake
    This gives your baby the chance to soothe themselves and learn to associate the cot (rather than you) with going to sleep. If your baby won't settle, try the patting technique, but try to stop before your baby goes to sleep.
  • stressing the difference between night and day
    Use dim lighting and keep interaction to a minimum.
  • starting a feed, play and sleep routine
    This could include a predictable sleep routine such as quiet time, followed by a story and then a cuddle before each sleep. At night time this routine could also include a bath.
