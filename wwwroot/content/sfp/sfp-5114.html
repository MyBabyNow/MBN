Safe sleep tips

You can reduce the risk of sudden unexpected death in infancy (SUDI) including sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS) and fatal sleeping accidents by following these tips.

  • Always put your baby to sleep on their back, never on their side or stomach
    This is the safest position. Once your baby learns to roll (4-6 months), continue to put them to sleep on their back but let them find their own sleeping position.
  • Make sure your baby's head can't get covered while sleeping
    Put your baby to sleep with their feet close to the end of the cot and tuck in blankets securely or use a sleeping bag. Sleeping bags with fitted head and arm holes are the safest option.
  • Avoid smoking
    Exposing your baby to second-hand smoke still harms them and the link between smoking and SUDI exists even when you smoke away from your baby. If you would like to quit but need some help check out Quitnow.
  • Don't use cot bumpers, soft toys, pillows, doonas and soft sleeping surfaces when your baby goes to sleep
    These items can suffocate babies.
  • Use cot that meets the current Australian standards
    Cots that meet the standards will be labelled (AS/NZS 2172:2003, or AS/NZS 2195:1999 for portable cots).
  • Use a firm, well-fitted mattress and sheets
    Make sure there is no gap between the mattress and the edge of the cot where your baby's head might get stuck.
  • Avoid letting your baby sleep on couches and makeshift bedding
    This is dangerous because your baby may get caught between pillows or cushions, a wall and a mattress or slip under blankets which may lead to suffocation.
  • Dress your baby in sleep clothes that are warm but not hot
    Overheating is a risk factor for SUDI including SIDS and fatal sleeping accidents.
  • Share a room
    Have your baby in a cot in your room for the first 6-12 months.
  • Make sure your baby's carer knows the safe sleep practices above
    Never assume grandparents or even professional child carers will know the above safe sleep practices.
