Parents provide, babies decide

What is parents provide, babies decide?
As a parent, your baby relies on you to provide what to eat, as well as when and where but it is up to your baby to decide how much to eat.

Parents:

Provide what to eat by:

  • serving a variety of healthy foods
  • offering new foods alongside their baby's favourites
  • preparing the same foods for everyone in the house
  • allowing their baby to explore foods

Provide when to eat by:

  • offering feeds/foods in response to their baby's hungry signs (this generally develops into more of a meal time routine as babies gets older)
  • limiting the amount of snacks between meals so their baby is hungry enough to try new foods, not just their favourites

Provide where to eat by:

  • making meal time = family time

sitting down together without distractions such as the TV or other screens

Babies:

Decide how much to eat by:

  • showing their hungry/fullness signs which can also include signs specific to solids
  • taking breaks from eating when they feel like it (which may also be a sign of being full)
  • exploring their food by feeding themselves
  • sometimes only eating some of the foods offered, rather than all (find how to handle fussy eating)

Remember, your baby's food intake will change from day-to-day, so it's better to only offer small serves and give more if needed rather than forcing your baby to finish all the food offered.
