Preparing and storing homemade baby food

Practise good hygiene

Your baby's immune system is still developing so tummy upsets can easily happen.

Reduce this risk by:

  • always washing your hands well with soap and water (antiseptic rubs don't always kill all the germs) before touching anything for food preparation
  • making sure any equipment to be used (e.g. knife, chopping board, bowl) has been washed well
  • not letting raw foods come into contact with cooked foods
    • always throwing out any leftover food your baby has not eaten from their plate as this can grow harmful bacteria
    • following the manufacturer's instructions for sterilising milk bottles
    • never reheating foods more than once

Baby food is easy to make!

There's no need to buy packaged food, fancy ingredients, special equipment, or take lessons on how to make food for your baby - just follow these simple steps!

No fancy equipment needed!

A food processor or blender is great for pureeing or mashing your baby's food when first introducing them to solids but if you only have a masher or fork, they are fine too, depending on the texture your baby can tolerate. Add a little liquid (e.g. breastmilk, formula or boiled cooled water) to make the food smoother.

No need to take cooking lessons!

Simply bake, steam, roast or microwave until foods are soft and tender. Just be careful when microwaving and stir wherever possible to avoid 'hot spots' that could burn your baby's mouth or tongue.

No special ingredients needed!
For the most part, you can save yourself time and money by making your baby's meal from the same ingredients as your own family meal.

Just remember:

  • that your baby's first solid foods should be high in iron
  • to remove your baby's serving before you add any sugar, salt (including stocks), extra fats and oils
  • you may need to mash or puree their food to begin with but as their chewing improves you can change the consistency to chopping up instead
  • to make sure any fruits and vegetables have been washed well

Preparing the right texture

As well as different types of foods, a variety of textures helps teach your baby different skills such as chewing and swallowing, which also eventually helps them with speaking.

It takes time to learn how to manage different textures, so it's important that the solids you offer are always prepared to a suitable consistency for your baby.

Start with pureed/finely mashed food before moving onto lumpier food, and finger foods (from 8 months). You should ideally do this before 10 months to avoid feeding difficulties down the track such as only accepting smooth or soft foods.

Purees/finely mashed food

Your baby's first solids should include foods high in iron and be prepared to a smooth texture.

Use a blender and add breastmilk, made up formula or boiled cooled water to make the texture thinner.

Lumpier food

Once your baby has mastered pureed foods (and is swallowing more than they are spitting out!) it's time to introduce lumpy food.

Instead of using a blender to puree the food, this time use a masher so the food is lumpier rather than completely smooth. This will help teach your baby how to chew their food and move their tongue. If you need to make the texture thinner, add some breastmilk, made up formula or boiled cooled water.

A good way to start is by mashing banana or avocado with a fork but don't forget that your baby still needs foods high in iron.

Extra baby food can be stored

Babies only tend to eat small amounts in a meal, so making extra food ahead of time is a great way of saving you valuable time and energy!

You can do this by:

  • making extra at a meal or preparing a large batch of food
  • freezing any extra food made (or leftovers not served) by putting 1-2 tablespoons of pureed/mashed food in separate blobs on a baking sheet and covering with cling film, or separating into:
    • airtight containers
    • ice cube trays sealed inside a plastic bag (e.g. snaplock bag)
  • transferring the cubes/blobs to a plastic freezer bag once the food is frozen solid
  • labelling the freezer bag with the contents and date it was prepared - this is important because frozen baby food should be used within one month of freezing

When you're ready to use frozen baby food, simply thaw the food in a closed container (not in an uncovered bowl) in the fridge.

If you need food ready more quickly, you can:

  • fill a large bowl with hot water and put the cube/blob in a small bowl sitting in the hot water - it should be ready in 10-20 minutes
  • use a microwave (as a last resort), making sure you stir the food well to avoid 'hot spots' that could burn your baby's mouth or tongue
