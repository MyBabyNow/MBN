Managing sleep
One of the biggest challenges of being a new mum is getting enough sleep. Your baby crying and needing feeds throughout the night can make it near impossible to get your full 7-8 hours overnight, but it is quite possible to get adequate sleep if you have at least one sleep during the day.

People often think that formula-fed babies sleep better than breastfed babies, but formula-fed babies also wake up during the night. In fact, some research shows that breastfeeding mums can get more sleep than formula feeding mums because breastfeeding in the middle of the night is much quicker and easier than getting up to prepare a bottle!

Some tips for managing sleep as a breastfeeding mum:

  • Offer your breast more during the day, or try 'cluster feeding' in the evening (giving your baby extra breastfeeds for a few hours) - this helps some babies sleep longer at night (but not all).
  • 'Dream feed' your baby or wake them up to feed before you go to bed - note they might still have a full tummy if it has been less than 3 hours since they last fed.
  • Ask a family member or friend for help with housework or looking after your baby - spend the extra time napping instead.
  • Take turns for night time duties with your partner - they bring the baby to you for a feed, do the nappy changing and re-settling following a feed so you can get back to sleep quicker.
  • Do shifts with your partner - your partner could give a bottle of expressed breastmilk during the night.
    • note that your body might stop making milk at this time of day (and reduce your supply) if you do this regularly
    • to avoid reducing supply do an extra pumping session after your long sleep stretch (or you might even wake up to express during your extra sleep time if your breasts are too full!)
  • Follow some sleep rules to maximise your sleep - make sure your room is dark and quiet and try to avoid:
    • caffeinated drinks (such as coffee, colas and tea) late in the afternoon and evening
    • using screens (TV, computers, mobile phones) around bedtime

Managing time

Taking care of your baby will take up the majority of your day, so learning to manage your time is a useful skill for new mums to master!
