Breastfeeding

Using dummies and bottles
It's best to avoid using a dummy or bottle until after 6-8 weeks to ensure you and your baby have a chance to get breastfeeding fully established first. This will also help to make sure you are feeding often enough to establish your milk supply.

If using a dummy or bottle, it is best to:

  • only use a dummy when you know your baby is not hungry, such as after feeds and at sleep time - a calm or sleeping baby doesn't need a dummy
  • not use a dummy to delay a feed when you are still setting up your milk supply - your baby needs to feed often in the first few weeks
  • choose a bottle teat that your baby needs to suck on, or a 'slow-flow' teat
  • never dip it in sugar, jam, honey or other sugary substances - this can cause your baby to gain too much weight and decay their teeth, even before they have come through
  • avoid giving your baby a dummy or bottle that has been in your mouth - this can spread bacteria that cause tooth decay
