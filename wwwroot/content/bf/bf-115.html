How often, how long and night feeds

How often should you breastfeed?

The feeding patterns of newborns vary a lot and the amount your baby feeds can also vary from day-to-day. Feeding your baby when they seem hungry and for as long as they want to feed, is best and also ensures your body makes enough milk to meet their needs. During the first 6 weeks it's especially important to follow your baby's lead as you set up your milk supply.

Trying to make your baby 'fit' into a specific routine or 'spreading out' the feeds in the early weeks and months:

  • can be stressful
  • may not suit your baby
  • will mean you make less milk

Remember:

  • your baby only has a small tummy but it digests your breastmilk very easily, so your baby needs to be fed often. It is normal for a breastfed baby to be fed 8-12 times a day in the first months. Feeding at least 8 times in a day helps maintain milk supply.

How long should you breastfeed?

Every baby is different and the length of time they feed for can also change from day-to-day and even feed-to-feed. Some babies linger, while others gobble and go!

You don't need to restrict how long you feed, you just need to make sure your baby feeds well and empties your breast(s) so they get all the creamy milk at the end of a feed. This helps keep up your milk supply

Babies usually get faster at feeding as they get bigger and better at it - newborns can take up to an hour, while older babies may take just 5 minutes!

Night feeds

Night feeds are important for setting up milk supply and for your baby's growth. Up to about 3 months old almost all babies will feed during the night. By 3 to 6 months old, more than half of all babies still feed through the night. After 6 months babies that are developing well no longer need to feed overnight. Read more about dropping night feeds.

Remember - babies' sleep patterns are different to ours as adults - even 'sleeping through' for a baby is defined as just 5 hours of continuous sleep.
