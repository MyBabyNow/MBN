Helpful products
The only equipment you'll need to breastfeed is your breasts and your baby, but there are many products on the market that you may find helpful. Some of these are listed below along with cheaper alternatives (note: My Baby Now does not endorse any one particular brand, even if branding is visible in some pictures).

Breastfeeding pillow
What: firm, high pillows that help support your baby to stay level with your chest when feeding.

Instead: try using a thick pillow you already have lying around the house.

Breastfeeding cover
What: something that covers you when breastfeeding in public.

Instead: try draping a scarf or shawl you already have over your baby when you're feeding.

Nursing (or maternity) bra
What: nursing (or maternity) bras have a small clip at the front to give easier access to your breast when you need to feed your baby or express milk. Find them in most places that sell underwear (including large budget department stores).

Nursing (or maternity) clothes
What:nursing (or maternity) clothes can provide discreet access to your breasts when you need to feed, which can make breastfeeding in public feel much easier! Find them in most department stores or buy them online.

Instead:sort through your clothes to find those that are breastfeeding-friendly, like a big cardigan that can cover your exposed breast. Another way is to layer with a singlet underneath to hide your tummy when you want to be more discreet.

Breastpads (nursing pads)
What:breastpads help absorb any leaking between feeds. They can be disposable or cloth (reusable) and different brands can have different absorbencies. Reusable ones might save you money in the long run!

Nipple cream
What:creams that aim to soothe the nipples. Some don't even need to be washed off between feeds.

Instead:try rubbing a little breastmilk over your nipples for the same soothing effect - it has anti-bacterial and healing properties that nipple creams don't have.

Breast pumps
What:breast pumps help express milk from your breasts and can be manual or electric (or you can just use your hand!). Manual pumps are cheaper than electric pumps but electric pumps can be hired as well as bought outright. Read more about breast pumps and how they work.

You do not need to purchase a breast pump, it is perfectly normal to breastfeed without one.

Instead:Learn how to hand express your breastmilk.

Breastmilk storage bags or trays
What:pre-sterilised bags (made from plastic thicker than regular plastic bags) to store your breastmilk in the fridge or freezer.

Storage trays are similar to ice trays but have lids and can be stacked in the freezer.

Bottles and teats
What:bottles and teats for feeding your baby. There are many on the market but try to use 'slow-flow' teats - learn why and how to find the right bottle and teat.
