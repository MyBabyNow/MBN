2.

Why do you only intend to breastfeed for a few days? (choose all that apply)

Don't be put off breastfeeding just because you intend to work. There are many ways you can continue to breastfeed once you're working and lots of mums actually find that doing so helps them to feel connected with their baby, despite being apart more often. Read more about options for breastfeeding when you're a working mum.
If you've had trouble breastfeeding a previous baby, it's completely understandable that you might feel anxious about breastfeeding your new baby. Keep in mind that every baby is different, so even if you had problems before, it doesn't mean you will have them again with this baby. And if you do, don't worry, plenty of help is available! Read more on how to make enough milk, getting your baby to attach well and some other common breastfeeding issues.
Being a new mum can be exhausting, especially when you're feeding around the clock! But given all the benefits that breastfeeding provides, it's worth sticking with it for as long as you can. There are plenty of things that don't involve feeding that your partner and others can do to support you and even though your old sleep patterns may no longer be possible, there are a lots of ways to make sure you still get enough sleep. Once your breastfeeding is established (6-8 weeks), you could also try expressing so someone else can give the occasional feed. And if you are worried about your partner missing out on bonding with your baby, don't be, there are plenty of other ways for your partner to bond with your baby that don't involve feeding.
If the thought of feeding your baby in public makes you feel nervous, boost your confidence with these tips and remember, you don't have to ask anyone if you can breastfeed or express milk in public - it is legal anywhere in Australia.
If most of your friends and family members have bottle fed their babies, it might feel like you should too. But if you want to breastfeed, there are plenty of ways to surround yourself with other mums who are breastfeeding for support, for example - join our forum, a mothers group in your local area (or online) or an Australian Breastfeeding Association support group in your local area. Connecting with other breastfeeding mums can make a world of difference. Remember, breastfeeding is a learning curve, we're in it together.
